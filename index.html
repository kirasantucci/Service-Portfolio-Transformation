Service Division Strategy

Phase2 Service Portfolio Transformation

Unlocking margin and efficiency through strategic portfolio management and targeted agent-driven delivery pilots.

Portfolio Organization: We've organized our service divisions by product type, offering, and/or delivery model. This enables us to identify and systematically solve margin challenges—starting with Shared Services, where we can establish replicable processes and automations that yield immediate impact. By first improving efficiency in Shared Services, we create a scalable set of agents and tools that we can then deploy across Project-Based delivery, Enterprise MRR, and other divisions. This structure positions Phase2 to compete on both financial performance and commercial innovation across diverse client needs.

Shared Services Pilot
Shared Services Transformation to 63% Margin
Agent-powered operating model targeting Q4 2026 | Current: 58.3% → Target: 63.0% | Gap: $134,706
The Opportunity: Shared Services clients require hands-on delivery but operate under constrained budgets. They can't retain dedicated teams, creating a bottleneck—requests pile up, context-switching taxes our team capacity, and throughput suffers when every task requires human conversation and execution. Our solution: embed agents into the workflow to consolidate intake, eliminate pre-dev overhead, and unlock deeper strategic engagement. This transforms Shared Services from a margin drain into a model of efficiency—one we can replicate across Shared Services Wave 2, Project-Based, and beyond. Clients receive the level of service that we hold ourselves to elsewhere in the business.
The Agent Operating Model: The Agent Operating Model orchestrates requests across multiple intake channels, intelligently routes them through specialized agents (intake, triage, docs, development, and strategic growth), and maintains human oversight at critical quality gates. This ensures we gain the efficiency of automation while preserving the judgment and client context that define great delivery.
